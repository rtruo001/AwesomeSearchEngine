MEASURE FOR MEASURE
a play by William Shakespeare

Morality has become lax in the city. Duke Vincentio leaves and appoints Angelo to rule in his stead. Angelo immediately decrees the immorality laws will be strictly enforced to save the souls of all. One of his first acts is to sentence Claudio to death for impregnating his fiancé Juliet. Isabella, a postulant at the nunnery and Claudio’s sister, runs to his aid. Angelo informs that he will spare Claudio’s life is Isabella spends the night in his bed.

Meanwhile, the Duke has disguised himself as a monk and is roaming the city to see how it fares under Angelo’s rule. While visiting the prison, he learns of Isabella’s and Claudio’s plight. He quickly develops a plan. He instructs Isabella to agree to Angelo’s terms. However, a woman named Mariana will be sent in her place. Mariana was engaged to Angelo years before, but he rejected her when her father’s fortune was lost at sea.

At the play’s conclusion, it is revealed that Angelo bedded Mariana and they will marry. Claudio is pardoned and reunited with Juliet. The Duke, overwhelmed with Isabella’s devotion and integrity offers his hand to her.

WILLIAM SHAKESPEARE (playwright) – Shakespeare is the ultimate examination for an actor. He is imaginatively bolder, takes greater risks in terms of character and narrative, explores the emotional and psychological parameters of a situation more comprehensively than anyone else, and all this in a language of either poetry or prose as compressed, exciting, and complex in thought and feeling as anywhere else in the English language.

    - Daniel Massey, veteran of the Royal Shakespeare Company
      The Duke in the 1983 production, directed by Adrian Noble
