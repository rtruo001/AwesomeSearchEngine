UCSD Radiation Safety Program

UC San Diego's institutional Radiation Safety Program, includes authorization required to conduct research, clinical, and teaching activities with radioative materials, employee and institutional responsibilities, operational standards, and waste management procedures.

UCSD Radiation Safety Manual

UC San Diego uses radioactive materials under a Broad Scope Radioactive Material License issued by the State of California. This manual represents the radiation safety program for all locations on that license, including the campus, Medical Center, and Scripps Institution of Oceanography. This manual is incorporated by reference into UCSD’s license. All principal investigators and radioactive material users are required to comply with all of its provisions, as well as the conditions of the Radioisotope Use Authorization (RUA).
